White Nights by Fyodor Dostoevsky

A short romantic tale set in St. Petersburg, focusing on loneliness,dreams, and unfulfilled love. Dostoevsky beautifully captures the inner emotional world of a solitary narrator who briefly experiences hope and human connection.

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Published: 1848

The Metamorphosis by Franz Kafka

A surreal and haunting story about Gregor Samsa, who wakes up one morning transformed into a giant insect. Kafka explores themes of alienation, identity, family responsibility, and the absurdity of modern life.

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Published: 1915

Kafan by Munshi Premchand

A powerful Hindi short story portraying extreme poverty, social injustice, and human helplessness. Through the tragic lives of a father and son, Premchand delivers a harsh critique of societal indifference and moral decay.

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Published: 1936

Books from My Reading List

This list includes classics, self-help, philosophy, and powerful literary works that explore human psychology, society, ambition, and morality.

  1. Crime and Punishment - Fyodor Dostoevsky
    A psychological exploration of guilt, morality, and redemption through the mind of a troubled young man.
  2. Gaban - Munshi Premchand
    A powerful novel about greed, desire, and the consequences of material obsession.
  3. Neglected Poems - Gulzar
    A collection of reflective, understated poems capturing loss, memory, love, and silence.
  4. The Monk Who Sold His Ferrari - Robin Sharma
    A motivational story about redefining success, purpose, and inner peace.
  5. Eat That Frog! - Brian Tracy
    A practical guide to overcoming procrastination and mastering productivity.
  6. Coffee Can Investing - Saurabh Mukherjea
    A long-term investment philosophy focused on patience, quality, and compounding wealth.
